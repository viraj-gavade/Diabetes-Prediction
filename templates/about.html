Dataset & Prediction Process

About the Dataset

This application uses the Pima Indians Diabetes Dataset, which contains medical and diagnostic measurements for 768 individuals, focusing on females of Pima Indian heritage.

Key Features:

  • Pregnancies: Number of pregnancies
  • Glucose: Plasma glucose concentration (mg/dL)
  • Blood Pressure: Diastolic blood pressure (mm Hg)
  • Skin Thickness: Triceps skin fold thickness (mm)
  • Insulin: 2-Hour serum insulin (mu U/ml)
  • BMI: Body mass index (kg/m²)
  • Diabetes Pedigree Function: Genetic score of diabetes
  • Age: Age in years

How the Prediction Works

Our diabetes prediction model follows these steps:

  1. User inputs their health metrics through the web interface
  2. Data is preprocessed and standardized using our ML pipeline
  3. The CatBoost classifier analyzes the features to calculate diabetes risk
  4. Results are presented with an explanation of what they mean
Note: This tool provides an estimate of diabetes risk and is not a substitute for professional medical advice. Always consult a healthcare provider for proper diagnosis and treatment.

Data Insights

Our analysis revealed key insights about diabetes risk factors:

  • Glucose level has the strongest correlation with diabetes outcome
  • BMI and Age are significant predictors of diabetes risk
  • The Diabetes Pedigree Function helps capture familial influence on risk
  • Combinations of these factors provide more accurate predictions than any single factor alone

Model Performance & Visualizations

Machine Learning Model Selection

After evaluating multiple machine learning algorithms including Random Forest, SVM, XGBoost, and CatBoost, the CatBoost classifier emerged as the top performer with the following optimized hyperparameters:

learning_rate = 0.05
l2_leaf_reg = 9
iterations = 300
depth = 4

These parameters were determined through extensive hyperparameter tuning using RandomizedSearchCV with cross-validation to ensure optimal model performance and generalizability.
